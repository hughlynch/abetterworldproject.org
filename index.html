A Better World Project

Civic technology built on proven infrastructure.

The most powerful ideas in technology — search relevance, reputation systems, collaborative filtering, distributed version control — were never inherently extractive. They became centralized because the economics of the early internet demanded it.

Those conditions no longer hold. The algorithms that power the largest platforms can today run at community scale, at trivial cost. A Better World Project is building the distributed version — structured so the value flows back to the communities that generate it.

What we're building

Weave — The Social Fabric

A digital environment that grows with a person from infancy through adulthood, governed at every stage by the institutions that already hold responsibility in that person's life. No engagement optimization. No infinite scroll. No algorithmic amplification. A social utility, not an attention casino.

Weft — Community Operating System

GitHub for your community. A readable portrait of how your town actually works, an issue tracker for community concerns, a proposal system modeled on pull requests, and a project tracker for civic initiatives. As the network grows, communities learn from each other.

Cubby — The Voice

AI-powered local beat reporting. Cubby attends public meetings and produces clear, accurate coverage, filling the gap left by the collapse of local journalism. More than half of U.S. counties now have no local newspaper.

Warp — Economic Foundation

Local relevance ranking, fair-price auctions, and verified reputation — the same algorithmic ideas that power search and commerce, applied at community scale. Revenue circulates back to fund civic infrastructure.

Our approach

Privacy as architecture

Field-level encryption, egress policy engines, hash-chained audit logs. We cannot leak what we do not have.

Community sovereignty

Each community gets its own instance. Your data belongs to your community. The algorithms serve local needs.

Self-sustaining

Local commerce funds civic infrastructure. No advertising. No data sales. No perpetual dependence on philanthropy.

Everything runs on Grove, an open-source orchestration framework. The products are available at abwp.ai.
